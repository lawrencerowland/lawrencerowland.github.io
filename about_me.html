Project GPT Framework

One implicit framework in Lawrence's work is his concept of configuring project-specific GPT agents – essentially a blueprint for an AI-augmented project team. By defining GPT agents for various roles (planner, analyst, etc.) and having them collaborate, he outlines a model for "AI-first" project management.

This framework suggests projects could be run with a mix of human and AI role-players, where AI handles defined responsibilities under human oversight. It's a reimagining of project governance structure to include AI as active contributors rather than passive tools.

Implementation steps include:

  • Define AI Team Roles: Identify specific functions within the project that could benefit from AI assistance
  • Configure Specialized GPTs: Create custom GPT agents for each role with relevant domain knowledge
  • Establish Communication Protocols: Define how AI agents will interact with each other and with humans
  • Implement Human Oversight: Establish governance mechanisms for critical AI outputs
  • Continuous Learning: Feed project outcomes back into AI configurations to improve performance

Benefits of this approach include reduced administrative burden, 24/7 project support, improved information flow, scalable project management capabilities, and standardized approaches to common tasks.

Two Types of AI Agents

Lawrence classifies emerging AI agents into distinct categories that help project managers understand their potential applications:

Thinker Agents: AI systems that can operate asynchronously – an AI that "can go away and think for you and message you later." Thinkers excel at deep analysis, complex problem-solving, and generating insights from large volumes of information.

Project applications include:

  • Analyzing historical project data to identify patterns and risks
  • Monitoring project metrics and alerting when intervention is needed
  • Generating comprehensive reports and summaries overnight
  • Conducting scenario analysis and what-if planning

Builder Agents: Action-oriented AI systems that can create tangible outputs such as code, applications, or structured content. Builders take goals and generate functional artifacts that support project work.

Project applications include:

  • Creating dashboards and visualization tools from project data
  • Developing specialized applications for project-specific needs
  • Automating routine workflows and processes
  • Generating structured documentation and deliverables

By distinguishing these agent types, Lawrence provides project leaders with a framework to identify what kind of AI assistant they need for different project challenges.

Early Adoption Flywheel

Lawrence predicts a compounding benefit to adopting AI in projects early – the "flywheel effect." In this view, organizations that start integrating AI now will generate data and lessons that feed into improving their AI models and processes, which in turn leads to better project outcomes, more data, and so on.

The flywheel consists of these stages:

  1. Initial AI Adoption: Organizations begin using AI tools for specific project tasks
  2. Data Generation: These implementations produce data about what works and what doesn't
  3. AI Refinement: Organizations refine their AI implementations based on this data
  4. Expanded Applications: As confidence grows, AI is applied to more complex tasks
  5. Competitive Advantage: The accumulated experience creates an advantage over organizations just beginning their AI journey

This framework serves as both a prediction and a persuasive model for change, implying that AI maturity will become a self-reinforcing advantage in project delivery.

Democratization of Project Management

Lawrence forecasts a more democratized project management landscape where AI tools make sophisticated project techniques accessible to a broader range of people. This prediction framework outlines how project management knowledge will be encapsulated in AI models that anyone can leverage, dramatically lowering the expertise barrier.

Key Elements of Democratization:

  • AI Project Assistants: Pre-configured AI assistants that encode project management best practices
  • Knowledge Encapsulation: Project methodologies captured in AI models with intuitive interfaces
  • Shared GPT Marketplace: Communities sharing specialized GPTs through platforms like the ChatGPT Store
  • Reduced Skill Threshold: Complex project tasks becoming accessible to those without formal training

As AI handles more technical aspects of project management, the human PM role will transform:

  • From scheduling and timeline management to strategic leadership and vision
  • From status reporting to stakeholder relationship management
  • From resource tracking to complex negotiation and conflict resolution
  • From risk identification to ethical judgment and critical decision-making

Ethical AI Governance Framework

On the cautionary side, Lawrence calls for frameworks to ensure AI is used responsibly in project management. He predicts that issues of AI ethics, transparency, and control will become central as adoption grows.

Key principles in his ethical governance framework include:

  • Transparency: All stakeholders should know when AI is being used in project processes
  • Validation: AI-generated outputs should undergo appropriate human validation
  • Alignment: AI systems must be configured to align with project and organizational goals
  • Equity: AI implementation should enhance project accessibility and not reinforce biases
  • Oversight: Clear governance structures should define who is accountable for AI-assisted decisions

This framework emphasizes that responsible AI adoption is not just about technical implementation but also about creating appropriate governance. It anticipates the need for formal policies as AI becomes more deeply integrated into project decision-making processes.

A Manifesto for Running Projects with AI in 2024

Principles for project delivery in the era of artificial intelligence

"We stand at the threshold of a transformation in how projects are delivered. Artificial intelligence offers unprecedented capabilities to augment human project teams, but requires thoughtful implementation guided by clear principles. This manifesto outlines the approach needed to successfully integrate AI into project delivery in 2024 and beyond."

— Lawrence Rowland

AI as Collaborator, Not Replacement

We believe that AI should augment human project teams, not replace them. The most effective project delivery will come from human-AI collaboration, where each contributes their unique strengths. AI excels at processing information, identifying patterns, and generating options; humans excel at judgment, creativity, and stakeholder engagement. Together, they form a more capable project delivery system than either alone.

Embrace Experimentation

We commit to active experimentation with AI in project contexts. Rather than waiting for perfect solutions, we believe in starting now with available tools, learning through hands-on application, and openly sharing successes and failures. This experimental mindset creates the "flywheel effect" where early adoption generates data and insights that improve future implementations.

Democratize Project Management

We strive to use AI to make project management more accessible to a broader range of people. By encoding project management knowledge and best practices in AI assistants, we can lower the barrier to entry for managing projects, enabling more people to deliver initiatives at various scales without requiring extensive formal training.

Prioritize Knowledge Flow

We recognize that project success depends on effective knowledge management. AI should be leveraged to enhance how information is captured, organized, retrieved, and applied within projects. Through techniques like Retrieval-Augmented Generation and knowledge graphs, we can ensure that project intelligence is accessible when and where it's needed.

Maintain Human Judgment

We insist on preserving human judgment for critical project decisions. While AI can provide valuable analysis and recommendations, the final responsibility for consequential choices should remain with human project leaders who understand context, values, and stakeholder needs that may not be fully captured in data.

Ensure Transparent Implementation

We commit to transparency in how AI is used within projects. All stakeholders should understand when AI is being applied, what role it plays in decision-making, and what safeguards exist for validation. This transparency builds trust and enables appropriate oversight of AI-assisted processes.

Focus on Value Creation

We implement AI to create tangible value in project delivery – whether through faster execution, higher quality outcomes, reduced costs, or enhanced stakeholder experiences. AI should not be adopted for its own sake, but should demonstrably improve how projects achieve their intended benefits.
